Chole Bhature

A popular North Indian dish featuring spicy chickpeas (chole)
served with deep-fried, fluffy bread (bhature).

Ingredients:

For chole:
  • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 2 onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
See more..
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
    • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji)
  • ½ cup yogurt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp baking powder
  • Oil (for deep frying)
Preparation Instructions:
Chole:
  • Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with salt until soft.
  • Heat oil, add cumin seeds, then onions. Sauté until golden.
See more..
  • Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for a minute.
  • Add tomato puree and spices. Cook until oil separates.
  • Add cooked chickpeas, mash slightly, simmer 10 minutes.
  • Garnish with coriander.
    • Bhature:
  • Mix all ingredients to form a soft dough, rest 2 hours.
  • Divide into balls, roll into circles.
  • Deep fry in hot oil until golden and puffed.
  • Serve hot with chole.

Chicken Pulao

A spicy and aromatic rice dish made with chicken, spices,
and basmati rice, popular in South India.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup basmati rice (soaked 30 mins)
  • 500g chicken (bone-in preferred)
  • 2 onions (sliced)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
See more..
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh mint and coriander
Preparation Instructions:
  • Marinate chicken with yogurt, salt, turmeric, and chili powder (30 mins).
  • Heat oil, sauté whole spices, then onions until golden.
See more..
  • Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for 1 min.
  • Add tomatoes, cook until soft.
  • Add marinated chicken, cook until 80% done.
  • Add soaked rice, 2 cups water, salt, mint, coriander
  • Cover and cook on low until rice is done.
  • Fluff gently and serve hot.

Pani puri

A beloved Indian street food consisting of crispy puris filled with spicy, tangy water and a savory potato-chickpea stuffing.

Ingredients:

For Puri:
  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt
  • Water (to knead)
  • Oil (for frying)
See more... For Filling:
  • 2 boiled potatoes (mashed)
  • ½ cup boiled chickpeas
  • Chaat masala, salt to taste
For Pani (Spiced Water):
  • 1 cup mint leaves
  • ½ cup coriander leaves
  • 1 green chili
  • 1 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • ½ tsp black salt
  • 1 tbsp tamarind pulp
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups cold water
Preparation Instructions:
Puri:
  • Mix semolina, flour, salt, knead stiff dough.
  • Rest 30 mins. Roll out and cut into small discs.
  • Deep fry until golden and puffed. Cool and store.
See more... Pani:
  • Blend mint, coriander, chili, tamarind, cumin, and salt.
  • Add to cold water, strain. Chill before serving.
Assemble:
  • Crack puri top, fill with potato-chickpea mix.
  • Add chilled spiced water. Serve immediately.

Aloo Fry

A simple and delicious side dish made with sautéed or fried potatoes and basic spices.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium potatoes (peeled and cubed)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
See more..
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Curry leaves (optional)
Preparation Instructions:
  • Parboil potatoes or soak raw cubes in water for 15 mins.
  • Heat oil, add mustard and cumin seeds.
See more..
  • Add curry leaves (optional), then drained potatoes.
  • Add turmeric, salt, chili powder.
  • Fry on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until golden and crispy.
  • Serve hot as a side with rice or roti.
